Behavior: counts consecutive identical bits. When the streak reaches the saved threshold N (regardless of whether the bits are 0s or 1s), the output z goes high. If the input flips before reaching N, the count resets to 1.

The FSM is laid out as two chains (one per streak value) plus a single INIT state. Both chains are identical in shape:

  • Top chain (red label): streaks of 1s. Feeding another 1 advances right; feeding 0 jumps back to position 1 of the bottom chain.
  • Bottom chain (blue label): streaks of 0s. Feeding another 0 advances right; feeding 1 jumps back to position 1 of the top chain.
  • INIT: only ever entered after reset. The very first bit you feed sends you to position 1 of whichever chain matches that bit.

The label inside each node is the streak's count — that's all the user sees. The chain a node belongs to encodes which bit is being counted, but that's purely internal plumbing for deciding when the streak breaks.
